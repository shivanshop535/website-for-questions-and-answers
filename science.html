Science Chapter 11: Cell Structure and Micro-organisms

1. Answer the following questions:

What is a cell?

Answer: The cell is the basic unit of life in living organisms.

Name the different organelles in a cell.

Answer: Nucleus, endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi bodies, lysosomes, mitochondria, vacuoles, plastids, chloroplasts.

What are micro-organisms?

Answer: Micro-organisms are tiny organisms that can only be seen under a microscope.

Which are the different types of micro-organisms?

Answer: Algae, fungi, bacteria, protozoa, viruses.

2. Fill in the blanks with the proper word:

The organelle called the ……………. is present in the plant cells only.

Answer: chloroplasts

Garbage is converted into ……….. by microorganisms.

Answer: fertilizer

In the cell, photosynthesis is carried out with the help of …………… .

Answer: chloroplast

An electron microscope is necessary for the study of ……………… .

Answer: micro-organism

The process of preparing their own food in presence of sunlight and chloroplast by plants cells is known as ……….. .

Answer: photosynthesis

3. What is the difference between us?

Plant cell and animal cell:

Answer: Plant cells have a cell wall, large vacuole, and chloroplasts, whereas animal cells do not.

Prokaryotic cell and Eukaryotic cell:

Answer: Prokaryotic cells lack a defined nucleus and other organelles, while eukaryotic cells have a defined nucleus and organelles.

4. Sketch and describe in your own words, the plant cell and animal cell:

Answer: Plant cells are structured with a cell wall and large vacuoles, whereas animal cells lack these structures and have multiple smaller vacuoles.

5. Explain the uses and the harmful effects of micro-organisms:

Answer:

Uses: Micro-organisms are used in fermentation, nitrogen fixation, waste decomposition, and vaccine production.

Harmful Effects: Some microbes cause diseases like malaria, typhoid, and food poisoning.

6. Give reasons:

Diseases spread on a large scale during periods of heavy rainfall and floods:

Answer: Floods contaminate water and create breeding grounds for mosquitoes.

There is a possibility of food poisoning if we eat stale food:

Answer: Microbes grow on stale food, releasing toxins that cause illness.

7. When will you use a simple microscope and when a compound microscope?

Answer: A simple microscope is used for observing fungi on bread, while a compound microscope is used to observe micro-organisms in labs.
